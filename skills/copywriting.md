# Copywriting — Sound Human

The #1 AI tell isn't visual — it's the copy. "Revolutionize your workflow with cutting-edge AI-powered solutions" is instantly recognizable as AI-generated. This skill teaches the agent to write like a human.

## The Process

### 1. Facts first
Before writing ANY copy, collect facts from PRODUCT.md:
- What does the product actually do? (Not what it "empowers" — what it does.)
- Who uses it? (Real people with real jobs, not "teams worldwide.")
- What changes for the user? (Concrete outcomes, not abstract benefits.)
- What does it cost? (If known. If not, don't mention price.)

If facts are missing, mark `[NEEDS INPUT]`. Never invent facts.

### 2. Write for one person
Don't write for "users" or "teams" or "everyone." Write for ONE person:
- A specific role (e.g., "an engineering manager at a 50-person startup")
- A specific moment (e.g., "standing up from a 2-hour status meeting that could have been a Slack message")
- A specific frustration (e.g., "spending Monday morning figuring out what everyone did last week")

### 3. Lead with outcome, not feature
- ❌ "AI-powered task automation with smart prioritization"
- ✅ "Stop spending Monday morning figuring out what everyone did last week"
- ❌ "Seamless collaboration across distributed teams"
- ✅ "Everyone sees the same board. No more 'did you update the ticket?'"

### 4. Use concrete language
- ❌ "Trusted by thousands of teams worldwide"
- ✅ "Used by engineering teams at 200+ companies" (only if true)
- ❌ "Boost productivity by 10x"
- ✅ "Ship 2 days faster per sprint" (only if measurable)
- If you can't prove it, don't claim it. Use qualitative claims: "Teams ship faster" instead of "47% faster delivery."

### 5. Short sentences. Active voice.
- ❌ "The platform is designed to help teams collaborate more effectively across distributed environments"
- ✅ "Your team sees the same board. Changes sync instantly."
- Maximum 20 words per sentence in hero subtext.
- Maximum 3 sentences per paragraph in body copy.

### 6. The headline test
A good headline answers: "What is this and why should I care?"
- ❌ "The Future of Project Management" (vague, grandiose)
- ✅ "Projects that actually ship" (specific, outcome-focused)
- ❌ "Unlock Your Team's Full Potential" (buzzword soup)
- ✅ "Stop herding cats. Start shipping." (conversational, specific)

### 7. CTA copy
Primary CTAs should be 1-3 words:
- ❌ "Get Started with Our Free Trial Today" → ✅ "Start free"
- ❌ "Schedule a Demo with Our Team" → ✅ "Book a demo"
- ❌ "Join Thousands of Teams" → ✅ "Try it free"

### 8. What to ban

**Words that are always AI slop:**
- Revolutionize, revolutionizing
- Cutting-edge, state-of-the-art
- Seamless, seamlessly
- Empower, empowering
- Unlock, unleashing
- Leverage, leveraging
- Synergy, synergize
- Next-gen, next-generation
- Game-changing
- Best-in-class
- World-class
- Robust, scalable
- Holistic, comprehensive
- Innovative, innovation
- Transformative
- Elevate, elevate your
- Curated (unless talking about an actual curated collection)

**Patterns that are always AI slop:**
- Em-dashes (—) in body copy — use commas or periods
- "Not just X, but Y" — just say Y
- "Whether you're a X or a Y" — pick one audience
- Three parallel phrases: "Fast. Secure. Reliable." — pick one
- Fake testimonials with real company names
- Fake statistics: "47% faster", "10,000+ teams"
- "Trusted by [real companies the user didn't mention]"

### 9. Style references

**Apple:** Reduction. One idea per sentence. Product IS the story.
> "iPhone 16 Pro. Built for Apple Intelligence."

**Linear:** Precise product argumentation. Technical but human.
> "Linear is a purpose-built tool for planning and building products. Streamline issues, projects, and product roadmaps."

**Stripe:** Clear explanation of complex things. No jargon.
> "Financial infrastructure for the internet. Millions of businesses use Stripe to accept payments."

These are principles, not templates. Don't copy their voice — find the voice that fits THIS product.

## Rules

- **Every claim needs a source.** If PRODUCT.md doesn't provide the fact, don't use it.
- **Read every string aloud.** If it sounds like a marketing email, rewrite it.
- **Shorter is better.** If you can say it in 5 words, don't use 15.
- **Specific > vague.** "Save 2 hours a week" > "Boost productivity."
- **One CTA per intent.** "Start free" and "Try it free" and "Get started" are the same intent — pick one label.
